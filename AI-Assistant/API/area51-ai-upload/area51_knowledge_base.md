## 2. Global booking and visit rules

- Online booking is strongly recommended/treated as essential. Walk-ins may be accepted, but availability is not guaranteed, especially on weekends and school holidays.
- Area 51 grip socks are mandatory on play equipment. Previously purchased Area 51 socks may be reused; other brands of grip socks are not accepted according to venue FAQs.
- A waiver is required for every participant. Customers can sign through their account or the link in the confirmation email.
- Guests are asked to arrive about 15 minutes before the booked session and present the emailed QR code.
- Ages 0–4 generally include one complimentary adult supervisor. Additional supervisors may be purchased.
- For ages 5–12, adults who participate on equipment require their own applicable ticket.
- Children must not be left unattended.
- Parental/adult supervision remains required; staff supervise equipment and rules but are not a childcare service.
- No participation with a cast. Many attractions also exclude people with specified injuries, heart/respiratory conditions, pregnancy, sprains or broken bones.
- Outside food is generally prohibited because of allergen transfer. Common stated exceptions are refillable personal water bottles, nut-free birthday cakes with applicable cakeage, and party bags intended to be taken home.
- Only nut-free cakes are allowed. General-admission cakeage is shown as $10 on venue FAQ pages.
- The venues state that they are wheelchair accessible. A carer accompanying a paid guest may enter free with a valid Companion Card.
- Lost property should be reported promptly by phone or email; venue FAQs state uncollected items may be disposed of after one week.
- Smoking and vaping are prohibited. Unsafe, abusive or bullying behaviour can result in removal without refund.

### Party booking rules
- A 50% deposit is required when booking.
- For weekend parties, final changes and the final invoice are generally due by Wednesday at 5:00 pm AEST. Add-ons and grazing boards must be added by the same deadline.
- All parties must be paid in full at least three days before the event; non-payment may lead to cancellation.
- The birthday child is included in the guest count.
- Siblings or other children outside the party package need their own pre-booked general-admission tickets.
- Supervising adults may observe free; adults who participate need the appropriate ticket.
- A standard pizza is described as 12 inches and eight slices.

- Guests may generally bring only a nut-free cake, candles, weighted balloons, take-home lolly/party bags and refillable water. No other outside food, smash cakes, confetti or party poppers.
- Parents/organisers should arrive about 30 minutes early and guests about 15 minutes early. Late arrivals lose play time because catering and room times are fixed.
- Waivers should be sent/completed before arrival, and RSVPs should be finalised around 10 days before the event.
- No-show guests are not refundable.
- Rescheduling with more than seven days’ notice may be allowed up to 12 months ahead, subject to availability. Within seven days, a $150 rescheduling/cancellation fee is stated; deposits and payments remain non-refundable. Requests must be made in writing.

## 13. Refund, cancellation and rescheduling

- All bookings are generally non-refundable and refund requests are considered individually by management; escalation does not guarantee approval.
- Refund consideration is described for government-mandated closure/restrictions or when Area 51 cannot provide the booking because of staffing, capacity, biohazard or similar restrictions.
- No refund, credit or transfer is required for change of mind, dislike/no use, no-show, or selecting the wrong date/time.
- A missed session is forfeited and is not rescheduled, refunded or credited.
- Play-pass cancellation or date/time change within 24 hours forfeits payment.
- Party deposits and payments are stated as non-refundable.
- Party cancellations within seven days are not eligible for refund and incur a stated $150 cancellation fee; rescheduling within seven days incurs a stated $150 fee.
- Party rescheduling with more than seven days’ notice may be allowed up to 12 months into the future, subject to availability.
- Party cancellation/rescheduling must be requested in writing; non-payment is not a cancellation request.

## 14. Safety summary

The official safety PDF contains attraction-by-attraction rules. Core rules suitable for an AI assistant:
- Children must not be left unattended and all participants must obey staff instructions.
- Area 51 socks are required on applicable equipment; shoes are removed for many play attractions, while enclosed shoes are required for Snow World and Astro Alley.
- Remove eyeglasses, jewellery and loose objects where instructed; tie long hair; wear suitable clothing.
- One participant per lane/wall/trampoline where specified; wait for landing/exit areas to clear.
- No rough play, racing, tackling, shoving, diving, flips, dangerous acts, food, drink, gum, glass or sharp objects on equipment.
- Harnesses, helmets, safety lines and restraints must be fitted/checked by staff where required and must not be tampered with.
- Participants should not exceed their ability and must meet attraction-specific age, height and weight limits.
- People with relevant back, neck, knee or joint injuries, heart/respiratory problems, pregnancy, sprains or broken bones are excluded from many attractions.
- Super Climber is commonly minimum 122 cm at Ipswich/Mt Gravatt/Redcliffe/Castle Hill.
- Underwood High Ropes: maximum 113 kg; 120 cm minimum alone; 110–120 cm with an adult helper aged 18+ according to the safety policy.
- Underwood Leap of Faith: minimum 120 cm, 20–90 kg.
- Underwood Climbing Arena: minimum 100 cm and 18 kg; maximum 113 kg.
- Underwood Trampoline Park: maximum 112 kg.
- Underwood Space Shuttle: ages 5–12, maximum 91 kg, no adults.
- Underwood Volcano: ages 5–12, minimum 100 cm, no adults.
- Ipswich High Ropes: minimum 122 cm, maximum 85 kg.
- Ipswich Climbing Walls: minimum 108 cm and 18 kg, maximum 130 kg.

### Helensvale operating hours
- **Issue:** The venue cafe page states play 9am–9pm daily, while the activities hub has shown 9am–7pm Sun–Thu and 9am–9pm Fri–Sat.
- **Recommended AI behaviour:** Use the venue-specific page or live booking page and verify date-specific hours.
### Redcliffe operating hours
- **Issue:** A hub summary has shown shorter hours than the Redcliffe-specific cafe page.
- **Recommended AI behaviour:** Prefer the Redcliffe-specific page; confirm public-holiday and special-event hours.
### Underwood High Ropes minimum height
- **Issue:** Some venue copy states 120 cm minimum, while the safety policy allows 110–120 cm with an adult helper aged 18+.
- **Recommended AI behaviour:** Answer with the full conditional rule and advise checking staff eligibility at booking.
### Group-booking deposit deadline
- **Issue:** Group pages show both '5 business days after booking' and 'within 7 days' for the 50% deposit.
- **Recommended AI behaviour:** Do not state one as definitive; direct the customer to the invoice or Groups team.
### Castle Hill group price for ages 1–2
- **Issue:** The page shows $13 and $18 in separate weekend/holiday rows without a clear duration on one row.
- **Recommended AI behaviour:** Mark as ambiguous and verify at checkout.
### Events and promotions
- **Issue:** What's On, Adults Only dates, giveaways, discount codes and ticket discounts change quickly and may remain indexed after expiry.
- **Recommended AI behaviour:** Do not treat as evergreen. Check the live page and current date before answering.
